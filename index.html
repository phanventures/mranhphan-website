About Anh

Real operator.
Not a guru.

I was born in Saigon and immigrated to Massachusetts at age 3. Growing up as the kid of Vietnamese immigrants in Worcester, I learned early that belonging isn't something that just happens — it's something you build, intentionally, for the people who don't already have it.

That shaped everything. The immigrant experience, the queer identity layered on top of all of it — none of it came with a ready-made community. You find your people, or you create the space where they can find each other. That's been the throughline of everything I've built.

I run Minnesota Dodgeball (leagues, tournaments, 300+ players), Always Funday (events and experiences), and Phan Ventures — all as a solo operator, while working a full-time day job. The only reason any of it works is systems: workflows, AI tools, and operational infrastructure that let me run multiple businesses without a team. That's what I teach.

The Phan Club is where it all comes together — the business building frameworks, the operational systems, and the honest reality of being a queer Vietnamese-American entrepreneur who's still very much in it.
